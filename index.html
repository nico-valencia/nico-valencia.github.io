Nicolas Valencia

Student Researcher at UC Berkeley

I am a recent graduate from UC Berkeley with a double major in geophysics and astrophysics. My research focuses on measuring azimuthal anisotropy in the southwest Pacific using station-side measurements from multiple body wave phase types, integrating observational and theoretical seismology. The results of this work will serve as constraints for the full-waveform tomography of the southwest Pacific, which is being developed within the Global Seismology Research Group.

I am currently seeking opportunities to advance my expertise, primarily through graduate programs where I can pursue a doctorate in seismology. I am particularly interested in applying my skills to better understand the interior structure of the Earth and other terrestrial planets, with the goal of contributing to our knowledge of planetary structure and evolution. Specifically, I am interested in studies of the lithosphere and asthenosphere, the coupling between deep Earth geodynamic processes and tectonic expression, and general advancements in geophysical inversion methods.
