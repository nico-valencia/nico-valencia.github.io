Nico Valencia

Geophysicist | Seismology Researcher | Data Scientist

I am a recent graduate from Berkeley with a double major in Geophysics and Astrophysics. I am interested in using techniques of geophysics, specifically observational and theoretical seismology, to research the interior structure of Earth and other planets. I currently am a data science intern for SoundThinking, where I am applying synthetic modeling and signal processing to support development of the SafePointe platform.

For my senior thesis, I worked with Dr. Barbara Romanowicz and the Berkeley Global Seismology Group on constraining shear wave splitting measurements for complex anisotropic media within the southwest Pacific. We showed that the incorporation of high-quality direct S phases with SKS phases through azimuthal binning improves measurements of Splitting Intensity across Australia and Indonesia. This novel Python workflow enables the measurement of receiver-side anisotropy for seismic stations with limited source distributions.

During my REU Internship at Brown University with Karen Fischer, we used Ps receiver functions to measure the structure of the Antarctic Ice Sheet. I developed MATLAB time-series analysis codes to better analyze temporal variations in ice properties, and demonstrated via synthetic receiver functions that we can resolve changes in ice thickness down to 200m using seismic body waves. We investigated heterogeneities in the ice and began development of a forward modeling scheme to optimize subsurface interpretation.

I have presented my work at multiple conferences, and have developed a strong foundation in geophysical research. I am currently looking for opportunities to continue my studies in seismology through doctoral programs starting Fall 2026. Please feel free to reach out me regarding my work, future plans, or anything!
